What is a FabLab?

A FabLab (short for Fabrication Laboratory) is a community workspace that provides access to tools, machines, and resources for:

  • Digital fabrication
  • Prototyping
  • Hands-on learning

Key Features

  • Open Access: Public/community use (often low-cost/free)
  • Digital Fabrication Tools:
    • 3D printers
    • Laser cutters/engravers
    • CNC machines (wood/metal/plastic)
    • Vinyl cutters
    • Electronics workstations (Arduino/Raspberry Pi)
    • Soldering & PCB-making tools
  • Software: CAD/CAM design tools (Fusion 360, Tinkercad, Inkscape)
  • Learning Focus: Workshops, tutorials, mentorship
  • Collaboration: Shared workspace for idea exchange

Origins & Purpose

  • Founded by Neil Gershenfeld at MIT's Center for Bits and Atoms (2000s)
  • Originated from course "How To Make (Almost) Anything"
  • Core Purposes:
    • Democratize technology access
    • Encourage innovation through rapid prototyping
    • Promote sustainable practices
    • Build creator communities

FabLab Activities

  • Prototype development
  • Custom design & fabrication (jewelry, furniture, art)
  • Robotics/IoT device creation
  • Technical skill development (coding, electronics)
  • Equipment repair/modification

Global Network

  • 2,000+ labs worldwide
  • Governed by Fab Charter principles
  • Open-source knowledge sharing
  • Global collaboration platform
